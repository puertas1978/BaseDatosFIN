ORGANIZATION OF INFORMATION AND SUPPORTING DOCUMENTATION DOCUMENTATION RELATING TO FINANCING OPERATIONS

Exporting companies, especially those whose main economic activity is productive, usually require financing all or a part of their operations with foreign capital. This is due to the strong volumes and cost of the first material and the lengthy time gaps between receiving an order and the actual collection of the export, factors that will otherwise require a huge availability of own resources, which, still available, would be unwise to devote.

Pixabay. Finance (CC BY-SA)

Example

A Spanish construction company working on international projects has a free treasury of 500 million euros, which could be used to acquire the materials and services necessary to satisfy multinational contracts that it obtains. Its order book consists of a construction of a viaduct in Chile, a highway in Brazil and a port infrastructure in Argentina, contracts that imply the need to assume costs worth 400 million each. There are two theoretical possibilities: to allocate the entire free treasury to only one of the projects and to renounce the other two, or, as good management indicates, take on a portion of the orders with own resources and finance the 700 million euros remaining through foreign resources

The problem raised is compounded when the company, because of its small size, has very limited resources. Because of this premise, companies with a view to internationalizing their activities must first access information related to possible financing, in order to be able to opt for the best conditions.
